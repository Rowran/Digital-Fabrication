Mold and Casting

Digital Fabrication Assignment 6

Mold and Casting Overview:

  • Synthesis / Define
  • Ideation / Develop
  • Test / Implementation / Deliver
  • Reflection

    • Synthesis / Define

    Design prompt criteria:
  • Criteria #1: Design and fabricate a mold in silicone.
  • Criteria #2: Use the mold to cast at least 4 identical parts (e..g in plaster).
  • Criteria #3: Design your master part in CAD first, then design your mold, then design a mold to cast your mold in.
  • Criteria #4: Fabricate your mold's molds either by CNC milling machinable wax or by 3D printing.

    • Ideation / Develop

    For this weeks project, I started off by considering making a mold with a functional purpose.
    Considering that fathers day is coming up, I decided that a Sextant mold would entertain my father and he could use it as a business asset.


    After I tried making a Sextant, I decided to keep it it simple so I downloaded a Sextant to act as the detailed model inside the Positive Mold. ( Sextant by IESSanchezCanton is licensed under the Creative Commons - Attribution license. )

    Test / Implementation / Deliver

    Initially I focused on learning the software to design my mold, utilizing Onshape for the Mold Base then Fusion 360 for the Sextant Model and for the assembly.
    This process enabled me to become much more familiar with Fusion 360.


    As per usual with Digital Fabrication, adjusting the model and fine tuning the output is a necessary portion of the process. The original plan was to learn more about subtractive cutting with Fusion 360, and the CNC tooling system.
    So I became familiar within the different modes such as Manufacturing adding new operations like manual tool changes between 3D milling operations.
    For example I learned how to convert the Stl models from Thingiverse using the Mesh to Bbodies function.




    Problem: Creating the Mold


    The plan was to cut the primary body of wax with a 1/8th" bit followed by a 1/32" bit to cut the intracies of the Sextant.
    However the projected cut time was acround 6+ hours.

    I iterated by switching over to 3D printing for quicker and more reliable results.
    This was the right decision but It create it own set of issues
    One Problem that I encountered with 3D Printing was that their was a kill point in the model's Gcode causing the print to cut itself off at a point.
    What I believe happened in the design was that in the Manufacturing tool mode I had a manual tool change operation.

    This bug was the primary reason why it failed two times. On the third print, the reel jammed causing a misprint. Fourth time the charm.
